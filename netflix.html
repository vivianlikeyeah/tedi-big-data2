Netflix

Netflix

Netflix users globally spend over 165 million hours watching content daily. But what has caused the success of new age streaming providers over traditional TV? Is it because we have more choice? Or are we feeding into a positive feedback loop pushed onward by big data analytics?

'Spoilt for choice' some might say when describing Netflix's selection. However, when more than 80 per cent of the TV shows and movies people watch on Netflix are discovered through the platform’s recommendation system, how much choice do you really have? What data are they using to inform these reccomendations?

Todd Yellin, Netflix’s VP of product innovation, told Wired in 2017: “what we see from those profiles is the following kinds of data — what people watch, what they watch after, what they watch before, what they watched a year ago, what they’ve watched recently and what time of day”

It's not just what show

Big Data is not just about the data - it's about the insight you derive from it! By examining a users online activity, streaming services can determines the most effective thumbnail for each user to entice them to click on a show. Streaming services also alter what snippet of the show to display, as a further means of tailoring their service to you, using your data.
