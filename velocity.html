The Five V's:Velocity

The Social Media Landscape

Everyone with a digital device can create data, in real-time. With just Facebook alone there are 10 billion messages, 4.5 billion times that the “like” button is pressed, and over 350 million new pictures are uploaded every day.

Velocity refers to how fast the data comes in. Data is no longer limited to a batch process. Data can be created, collected and transmitted in real-time.

Why has the velocity of data increased?

The velocity of data has increased for several reasons. Ultimately it comes to access and advancements. Consider the following: In 2010 there were 62 million smartphone users, able to contribute geo-locations, texts, calls, etc. Less than 10 years later in 2019 we have over 230 million smartphone users. Then if we compare the advancements in technology; the capabilities of data capture that a smart phone has over say a land-line. It is clear why the velocity of the data has increased.
