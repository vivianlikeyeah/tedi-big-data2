The most popular music streaming service currently on the market is Spotify. Spotify has over 217 million users, 3 times more than its leading competitor Apple Music that sits at a mere 60 million users. What is spotify doing differently? Other than adopting a freemium model, the key differernce between the two competitors is their algorithmic vs user curated models.

Spotify, and other streaming services similar to it, are able to present users with custom playlists based off their listening patterns; in a bid to determine the perfect soundtrack for you. Songs you've liked, Songs you've listened to, the number of times you've listened to a song are all used to determine the right playlist for you.


Why is this a game changer?

Big Data has transformed the music industry. Big Data is not about the sheer amount of information you can gather on each person's listening habbits, but more so about the insights that can be derived from it. Taking a look at the figure on the left, you can see the same playlist demonstrated in two user's spotifys. Why are they different? Given the data that spotify has obtained from each user, they have derived insight. Creating a vast number custom playlist for particular moods, events, even times of day. By giving a user exactly what they want, you keep them coming back for more.
