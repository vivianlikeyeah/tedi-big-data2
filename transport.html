Google Maps

Google Maps is a mapping service developed that has a range of features that leverage Big Data. Big Data is all about turning information into insight. How do mapping services use data to differentiate themseleves from your traditional maps? When a route is presented to you by google, there are several options that it could take. But how does it give you the fastest route? The best route? By Using real-time data, calculating the number of cars on the road based off the number of app users, google maps is able to determine the fastest route. The real time nature of this is heightened as the app is able to re-route during a trip based of real-time changes in traffic.

As a pedestrian you may notice outlines of buildings on the street. How does google create such renders? The company does so by algorithmically linking aerial imagery and computer vision to create building to extreme detail. Such creations assist heavily with on foot navigation, and thereby are turning data into insight.

Trip View

Australian public transport users have access to an application, provided by the government, called Trip View. Trip view provide real-time updates as to how far away your bus/train is (based off location, traffic, route etc). As well as this, Trip View can take this insight one step further, by leveraging data derived from the weight sensors on each bus and train trip view can tell you how full your bus is, or what carriage is the best to board. The real-time, and the analytics performed on it, not only improves an individuals commute, but also drastically reduces congestion throughout the city.
