The Five V's:Volume

The Large Synoptic Survey Telescope

The Large Synoptic Survey Telescope, is a telescope built to rapidly survey the night sky. It has a field of view of 10 square degrees of sky (40x the size of the full moon). This telescope generates 30TB of images every night.

The First Image of a Black Hole

Taking that one step further - Astronomers have successfully captured an image of the a black hole in a galaxy 500 million trillion km away. The resulting image is 5000 TB large! Can you imagine if we were capturing one of these images every night?

The most obvious of the five V's; Volume. Volume refers to the sheer amount of data. Each day over 2.5 quantillion bytes of data is created. We have gotten to the point where we can no longer manage and store the volume of data in a databases that you learnt about in Previous Node.In 2010,it was estimated that the world was “awash with over 800 exabytes of data and growing.”

Why has the volume of data increased?

Data is being produced by everyone! Businesses, consumers and machines. The increased amount of data sources, coupled with the increased size of data, results in a huge amount of data. Consider a .txt file compared to a .mp4 file. Then consider the billions of users who are able to upload these .mp4 files, compared to the thousands who previously had access to computers.
