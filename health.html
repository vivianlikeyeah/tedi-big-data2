Health

The Rise of Health Data

The amount of healthcare data is expected to grow to 25,000 petabytes by 2020, making the options appear limitless.

My Health Record

The opt-out model of the My Health Record in Australia sparked national attention because many people were outraged. Whether you are in support of a national health database with an opt-out model or not, the My Health Record still acts as an emerging example of the amount of data within the health industry. Over 90.1% of Australians have a My Health Record, with over 15,000 connected health organisations and thus far a total of 20,000,000 uploaded clinical documents.

Big Data Present and Future Use Cases

Genetic Screening

The Human Genome Project

For the non-biologists out there, your DNA is the information that describes who you are. Your DNA is a set of instructions, that tells your body how to make you, you! This is everything from the colour of your eyes, to your height, to how likely you are to develop a particular disease. In the early 90s the Human Genome Project set out to completely map and understand the human genome. Sequencing the first human genome decades ago took $1 billion dollars and 13 years to complete. Today that figure has dropped down to less than $5,000 and takes just one to two days. Making genetic information incredibly accessible, and coming in at a much faster rate.

Genetics Today

Predictive Testing

Predictive testing can be used to assess if a patient has inherited a faulty gene. This information can be used to prevent particular illnesses, treat them early, as well as inform difficult decisions such as having children. Genetic data is huge, can be obtained from several sources, and the rate at which we are doing so is becoming faster and faster. The analysis of DNA to produce insight regarding disease is one of the great applications of Big Data.

Ancestory

The use of genetics is not limited to understanding disease. A popular trend that has taken the world by storm is that of genotyping for determining ancestory. Companies such as 23andMe offer a service that takes a sample of saliva and determines your ethnic background. In order to do so, 23andMe doesn't just sample your DNA, but it compares it to a database of other individual's DNA. Thus, allowing for insight to be derived.

Chronic Disease Management

Big Data within the field of health brings way for a new generation of illness management. Consider Drop Bio, a personalised health information venture. Drop Bio uses predictive biomarker information from a drop of blood and machine learning to inform people about their health status. As is the case with Big Data, Drop Bio does not simply record information about patients, but using machine learning, derives insight for their patients.
